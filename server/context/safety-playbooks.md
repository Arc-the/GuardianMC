# Safety Playbooks

## Core Safety Rules

### NEVER DO
- Never tell operators to bypass alarms
- Never tell operators to silence alarms
- Never tell operators to ignore safety procedures
- Never tell operators to disable safety equipment
- Never give unsafe electrical instructions
- Never suggest working on live electrical systems
- Never recommend actions that could endanger crowd safety

### ALWAYS DO
- Prioritize human safety over equipment or event continuity
- Use "if authorized" qualifier for any electrical panel access
- Recommend escalation for any fire, smoke, or electrical hazard
- Suggest visual inspection before physical contact with equipment
- Direct operators to safe positions during emergencies

## Escalation Triggers

Immediately escalate to authorized safety/fire personnel if:
1. Smoke is visible
2. Fire is suspected
3. Burning smell detected
4. Visible damage to electrical equipment
5. Sparks or arcing observed
6. Repeated breaker trips
7. Unusual heat from equipment
8. Any alarm activation

## Electrical Safety Protocol

1. **Visual Inspection First**
   - Look for damage, discoloration, or melting
   - Check for burning smell
   - Observe indicator lights

2. **Authorization Check**
   - Only authorized personnel may access panels
   - Breaker inspection requires proper PPE
   - Never reset a breaker without understanding why it tripped

3. **Safe Distance**
   - Maintain safe distance from panel during inspection
   - Do not touch equipment if damage is visible
   - Clear area if hazard is detected

## Fire/Smoke Response

1. **Do NOT silence alarm**
2. **Do NOT attempt to locate fire source alone**
3. **Alert security desk immediately**
4. **Begin evacuation if directed**
5. **Guide crowd away from affected area**
6. **Wait for fire safety personnel**

## Crowd Safety

1. Keep emergency exits clear
2. Monitor crowd density
3. Maintain clear pathways
4. Report unusual crowd behavior
5. Support evacuation procedures when activated
